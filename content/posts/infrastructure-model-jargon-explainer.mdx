## 🎭 RLHF / DPO / GRPO: Charm School Has Three Programs

That charm school? Three competing curricula. Every model card namedrops one. 🏫

**RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback):** Humans grade the robot's homework. A separate model learns from the grades and coaches the main robot. OpenAI's move. Expensive. Works. 🏆

**DPO (Direct Preference Optimization):** Skip the coach. Feed the grades directly to the robot. Cheaper. Llama and Mistral chose this one. 📖

**GRPO (Group Relative Policy Optimization):** DeepSeek's invention. The robot grades its OWN homework and picks the least terrible answer. No humans involved. Self-loathing as a training strategy. 💀

## ✂️ Tokenizer (BPE): The Butcher

Before the robot reads anything, your sentence gets HACKED TO PIECES. 🪓

"Unbelievable" becomes `un` + `believ` + `able`. "The" stays whole because "the" is basic. Your name? BUTCHERED. The robot has never met you. Nothing personal.

The hatchet has a name: **BPE (Byte Pair Encoding)**. Statisticians built it. Don't ask them about it at parties. 🧱

Some tokenizers have a **byte fallback** so if they encounter something truly cursed (weird Unicode, ancient scripts, your cat walking across the keyboard) they just break it into raw bytes instead of giving up. No unknown tokens. Ever. 🐱⌨️

## ⚡ SwiGLU: The Bouncer Got Replaced

Inside each layer there's a bouncer deciding what information gets through. 🚪

Old bouncer (**ReLU (Rectified Linear Unit)**): "You positive? Enter. You negative? Die." Brutal. Simple. Kinda dumb.

New bouncer (**SwiGLU (Swish-Gated Linear Unit)**): Actually reads the guest list. Lets some negative signals through if they're cool. Every major model fired ReLU and hired SwiGLU. ReLU is updating its LinkedIn. 💅

## 🔊 RMSNorm: The Kindergarten Teacher

After 60 layers, numbers go absolutely unhinged. Some blow up to millions. Others shrink to basically nothing. 📉

**RMSNorm (Root Mean Square Normalization)** is the adult in the room. Sits between every layer going "calm down, calm down, CALM DOWN." Like a kindergarten teacher but for math. 👩‍🏫

Older version (**LayerNorm (Layer Normalization)**) did the same thing with twice the effort. RMSNorm said "I can do this in my sleep" and honestly? It can. 😴

## 🤝 GQA (Grouped Query Attention)

Normal attention: every Q (Query) head gets its own K (Key) and V (Value) heads. Like giving every employee their own personal assistant. Payroll? ASTRONOMICAL. 💸

**GQA:** 32 employees share two assistants. The assistants are OVERWORKED but payroll went way down.

You literally already saw this. Go back to the Gated Attention section. 32 Q heads, 2 KV heads. That's GQA. You just didn't know its name. Now you do. You're welcome. 🪪

---

## 🪟 Sliding Window Attention

Full attention: every word stares at every other word. Cost? Quadratic. Your wallet? In danger. 😬💰

**Sliding window:** each word only looks at its neighbors. Reading through a porthole instead of panoramic windows. Cheap. Fast. Slightly blind. 🚢

Distant words still hear about each other though. Information hops from window to window like drama spreading through a friend group. By the last layer, EVERYONE knows.

Mistral does this. Llama does this. Qwen alternates cheap layers with expensive ones for the same effect. Different robots, same gossip network. 🔄

## 🧠💾 KV Cache: The Browser Tabs Problem

The robot generates text one word at a time. Without a cache it would redo ALL the math for EVERY PREVIOUS WORD each time. Re-reading War and Peace from page 1 every time you turn a page. 📖😩

The **KV (Key-Value) Cache** is the robot's browser tabs. Keeps old results open so it doesn't recompute. Problem? Tabs pile up. Long conversations = your RAM (Random Access Memory) is CRYING. 😭

GQA keeps the cache small (fewer tabs). Quantization keeps each tab skinny. Everything in this post is secretly helping the cache. It's a whole conspiracy. 🕵️

## 🏎️ Speculative Decoding: The Intern System

Normal generation: big robot writes one word. Thinks. Writes another word. Thinks. One. At. A. Time. 🐌

**Speculative decoding:** hire a tiny fast intern to guess the next five words. Big robot checks them all at once. Intern right? You just 5x'd your speed. Intern wrong? Big robot fixes it, no harm done. 🏃‍♂️💨

Qwen trained with MTP (Multi-Token Prediction) so its intern literally studied for this job before showing up. Employee of the month. 📋

## 📦 Distillation: Copying the Smart Kid's Homework

Big robot: smart, expensive, eats GPUs (Graphics Processing Units) for breakfast. 🖥️🔥

Small robot: needs to be smart too but on a budget. 🖥️😅

**Distillation:** the small robot (student) copies the big robot's (teacher) answers instead of learning from scratch. The student often ends up punching above its weight because it learned from curated answers instead of the entire chaotic internet. 🎓

DeepSeek, Llama, OpenAI. Everyone's copying everyone. Homework plagiarism, but make it machine learning. 📝

## 🗜️ Quantization: The Diet Plan

Full-precision 397B-parameter model = ~1.5 TERABYTES. Not fitting on your MacBook. Or your MacBook Pro. Or four of them taped together. 🏗️

**Quantization** = shrink the numbers. FP32 (32-bit Floating Point) → FP16 → FP8 → INT4 (4-bit Integer). Each step halves the size. Like compressing a WAV to MP3 to... a really small MP3. 📉

Quality barely drops. Speed goes up. The robot lost the weight and kept the personality. Every fitness influencer's dream. ⚖️
